The Dukan diet is a high-protein, very-low-fat diet organised in **four phases** — from the
very strict (pure protein) to long-term maintenance. It was developed by French physician
Pierre Dukan and achieved extraordinary global popularity in the 2000s.

The mechanism: very high protein intake produces strong satiety, maintains muscle mass and
increases thermogenesis (the body uses more energy to digest protein than other macronutrients).
In the early phases, carbohydrates and fats are almost completely eliminated.

Despite commercial success, the Dukan diet is **controversial** in the nutrition community:
it is very restrictive, strains the kidneys with excess protein, and the long-term risk of
weight regain is significant. The French National Nutritional Health Programme explicitly
recommended against it as a dietary approach for the general population.

## History and origin

Pierre Dukan, a French physician specialising in neurology who later moved to nutrition,
developed the diet over several decades of work with patients with obesity. He began testing
high-protein protocols in the 1970s, inspired partly by Robert Atkins, but eliminated fat
from the equation — an aspect he considered problematic in the Atkins approach. He described
the protocol to patients informally for years before publishing it in book form.

The book *Je ne sais pas maigrir* (I Don't Know How to Get Thin) was published in France in
2000 and became a publishing phenomenon: it sold millions of copies in France and was
translated into 32 languages. The English version, *The Dukan Diet*, appeared in 2010 and
reached the top of bestseller lists in the UK and United States. Dukan claimed over
40 million followers worldwide.

The celebrity-endorsement moment came when it became publicly known that Kate Middleton and
her mother had followed the Dukan diet before the royal wedding in 2011 — a detail that
generated enormous global press coverage and drove a new wave of interest. In Romania, the
diet reached peak popularity in 2011–2013 through translations and local editions.

Dukan was subsequently controversial: the French Medical Association investigated him for
commercially promoting dietary practices, and in 2012 his name was removed from the official
register of doctors following a ruling by the French Medical Council — a rare and significant
sanction in the medical community.
