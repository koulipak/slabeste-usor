The carnivore diet is an **extreme** form of low-carb: it almost completely eliminates plant
foods and is based exclusively on animal products — meat, fish, eggs and, in some variants,
dairy. It removes carbohydrates from the diet entirely.

It is a highly controversial diet. It appeals through simplicity and satiety, but eliminates
fibre and a range of plant-derived nutrients, with high saturated fat intake and little long-term
evidence. We include it here for completeness — it is not an approach to recommend without
medical supervision.

## History and origin

The first systematic observations about a diet based exclusively on animal products came from
explorers who lived among Arctic peoples. Vilhjalmur Stefansson, a Canadian-American
anthropologist, spent years on his expeditions (1906–1918) eating exclusively meat and fat
alongside the Inuit and returned in good health. In 1928, together with a colleague, he
participated in a one-year controlled experiment in New York, eating only meat under medical
supervision, without suffering any manifest deficiencies. The study was published and fuelled
debates that continue today.

In the modern era, the carnivore diet was popularised mainly through non-academic channels.
Shawn Baker, an American orthopaedic surgeon, published *Carnivore Diet* in 2019 and built a
significant online community around his ideas. Canadian philosopher Jordan Peterson contributed
to the diet's notoriety after publicly recounting improvements in his health following the
adoption of a strict carnivore regimen. These personal testimonials generated considerable
interest but do not constitute clinical evidence.

Controlled long-term studies on the carnivore diet are almost completely absent from the
peer-reviewed literature. A few observational analyses and surveys of followers have been
published recently, but small sample sizes and non-randomised designs limit the conclusions.
The medical and nutritional community remains sceptical about the long-term safety and
sustainability of a regimen that completely eliminates fibre and plant foods.
