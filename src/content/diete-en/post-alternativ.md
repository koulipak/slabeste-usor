**Alternate-Day Fasting (ADF)** is a form of intermittent fasting that alternates between
**fasting days** (complete fast or maximum 500 kcal) and **normal eating days** (no caloric
restrictions). It is a more demanding variant than the 5:2 protocol, with 3–4 fasting days
per week.

The mechanism: weekly caloric deficit from fasting days produces weight loss, while normal
days make the protocol psychologically more sustainable than continuous restriction. Research
shows comparable results to standard caloric restriction when total weekly calories are
similar.

In practice, ADF is **difficult to maintain** socially and psychologically due to the
frequency of fasting days. The 5:2 protocol (5 normal days, 2 fasting days) is generally
considered more sustainable and has been more widely studied.

## History and origin

Alternate-day fasting entered the scientific literature through the work of Krista Varady, a
professor of nutrition at the University of Illinois at Chicago, who has published extensively
on ADF since the mid-2000s. Her early studies, published from 2007 onwards, demonstrated
that ADF produced weight loss and improvements in cardiovascular markers comparable to
continuous caloric restriction — findings that contradicted the assumption that daily
restriction was the only effective approach.

Varady's research popularised both the modified ADF protocol (500 kcal on fasting days rather
than a complete fast, to improve adherence) and the "every other day diet" framing. She
published *The Every-Other-Day Diet* (2013), making the research accessible to a popular
audience.

Interest in ADF grew alongside the broader 2012–2015 explosion of intermittent fasting
popularity, driven largely by the BBC documentary *Eat, Fast and Live Longer* (2012, featuring
Michael Mosley) and his subsequent book *The Fast Diet* on the 5:2 protocol. Varady's ADF
research provided some of the scientific foundation cited in this wave of popular interest,
though the 5:2 protocol ultimately attracted more followers due to its lower frequency of
fasting days.
