The **Cabbage Soup Diet** is a 7-day very-low-calorie programme in which unlimited
**cabbage soup** is consumed daily, with a different additional food allowed each day of the
week — fruit, vegetables, bananas and milk, lean meat, brown rice.

It is one of the most extreme short-term crash diets in circulation. The weight loss (3–5 kg
in 7 days) is real but almost entirely **water and glycogen**, not fat. After the 7 days,
normal eating rapidly restores the lost weight.

The diet has no credible scientific basis beyond simple caloric restriction — cabbage has no
magical fat-burning properties. We document it here because it is widely searched, not because
we recommend it.

## History and origin

The origin of the Cabbage Soup Diet is unclear. It began circulating in the United States in
the 1980s as a typed document passed around in offices — similar to the GM diet — sometimes
attributed without evidence to the Sacred Heart Memorial Hospital, the Mayo Clinic or
military hospitals. None of these institutions have confirmed any connection to the programme.

The diet spread through photocopying, then through email chains in the 1990s and internet
forums in the 2000s. Its viral mechanism is identical to other crash diets of the era: a
specific promise (7 days, specific weight loss), simple rules and an appeal to authority
(hospital attribution).

In Romania, the cabbage soup diet became known especially through internet forums and
women's magazines in the 2000s–2010s. The local cultural resonance of cabbage — a staple
in Romanian cuisine, cheap, accessible and associated with health in folk tradition — likely
contributed to its popularity relative to other crash diets. Nutritionists and dietitians
have consistently criticised it, but its simple appeal as a quick fix before events (weddings,
holidays) continues to generate recurring interest online.
