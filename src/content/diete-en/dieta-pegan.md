The **pegan diet** is a hybrid approach created by American physician Dr Mark Hyman,
combining the best elements of **Paleo** and **vegan** diets. The formula:
**75% plant foods** (vegetables, fruit, nuts, seeds, legumes in moderation) and
**25% quality animal protein** (grass-fed meat, wild fish, eggs).

It eliminates dairy, gluten, refined sugar and processed foods — elements both paleo and
vegan agree on — and resolves the main tensions between the two approaches through a pragmatic
compromise. Unlike strict paleo, pegan allows legumes in moderation. Unlike veganism, it
allows animal products in reasonable quantities.

The result is a diet that is **nutritionally rich and balanced**, practically sustainable
and without extreme restrictions. It is not designed as a rapid weight-loss diet but as a
sustainable lifestyle based on food quality.

## History and origin

Dr Mark Hyman, functional medicine physician and director of the Cleveland Clinic Center for
Functional Medicine, coined the term "pegan" in a blog post in 2014 — initially, he admits,
as a provocative joke about the absurdity of heated debates between paleo and vegan advocates.
He noted that, despite apparent opposition, both approaches share fundamental principles:
whole, unprocessed foods, no refined sugar, no dairy, no gluten.

The concept caught immediate attention and Hyman developed it into a comprehensive programme.
His book *The Pegan Diet* was published in 2021 and systematised the approach with detailed
guidelines, recipes and scientific evidence for the choices made. Hyman was already a
well-known author with multiple bestsellers on functional medicine and nutrition (*Eat Fat,
Get Thin*; *Food: What the Heck Should I Eat?*), which gave the pegan concept a large ready
audience.

The pegan diet fits into a broader trend of hybrid dietary approaches that seek to transcend
ideological divisions between plant-based and animal-based advocates, emphasising food
quality over categorical exclusion. It attracted particular interest among people who had
tried both paleo and vegan and found both too extreme, looking for a practical middle ground.
