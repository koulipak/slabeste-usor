Every time a celebrity loses visible weight, the public's first reaction is to look for the "trick": what expensive treatment, what injection, what exotic diet made the difference? In the case of Gabriela Cristea, the answer is almost disappointingly simple — and that's exactly why it's worth telling. The TV presenter lost around 25 kilograms without anything spectacular, and she herself insisted on this point.

Let's look at what she said publicly about how she managed it, what she actually eats in a day, and why her method, though unremarkable, is in fact the only one that works long-term.

## What she actually did: less on the plate

Gabriela Cristea was clear that she did not resort to miracle diets, expensive treatments, or quick solutions. Everything came down to a **low-calorie diet** — under approximately 1,000 calories per day during the weight loss period — and discipline. "I eat very little now," she said, adding a simple but accurate observation: you don't need the full amount of food you usually put on your plate.

Translated into practical terms, this means one thing: she reduced portions enough to enter a consistent [calorie deficit](/dieta/deficit-caloric/). It doesn't matter how fancy a diet sounds; if it makes you consume fewer calories than you burn, you lose weight. If not, you don't. Everything else is packaging.

An important detail: her motivation was not purely aesthetic. She shared that she had reached a point where "I could be in a risk category" — meaning a health reason, exactly the kind of trigger that keeps someone on track better than any promise of a slimmer figure.

## What a day in her diet looks like

The useful part for the reader isn't the number on the scale, but what she actually eats — because they're normal, accessible foods, not expensive ingredients from organic shops. From her public statements, a typical day looks roughly like this:

- **Morning:** yogurt with rye flakes, or mozzarella with tomatoes and a drizzle of olive oil.
- **Lunch:** salads and soups — food with high volume and few calories, which fills you up.
- **Evening:** seafood, sushi, grilled chicken, or polenta with yogurt.

Notice the logic behind it, even if she probably didn't think of it in technical terms: every meal is built around something satiating (protein or volume from vegetables) and avoids exactly the hidden caloric bombs — white bread, frying, sauces, sweets. There's no magic there. It's a moderate plate, repeated daily.

She added **exercise** to this — she mentioned doing a lot of it. The combination of smaller portions and greater expenditure is, after all, the classic formula, regardless of who applies it.

It's worth dwelling on her observation about the plate, because it's smarter than it sounds. Most of us eat "as much as was served," not "as much as we need" — the portion is dictated by the size of the plate and habit, not by real hunger. Using a smaller plate and stopping when you're no longer hungry (not when you've emptied it) cuts, effortlessly and without counting anything, several hundred calories per day. It's a mundane trick, but exactly the kind of invisible change that adds up to 25 kilograms over the course of a year.

## The myth her case debunks

The most persistent myth about celebrities who lose weight is that "they have resources we don't have" — personal chefs, trainers, inaccessible treatments. Sometimes that's true. But Gabriela Cristea's case shows exactly the opposite: a woman who lost weight eating yogurt with flakes, salads, and grilled chicken — things you have in any fridge.

The myth is dangerous because it provides a convenient excuse: "if only I had her money too…" The reality is that the main tool — portion control — is free. What costs, and genuinely can't be bought, is the **consistency** to apply it day after day, including the days you don't feel like it.

A second, subtler myth: the idea that you have to starve yourself to lose weight like her. Be careful here — 1,000 calories per day is a low threshold, possibly appropriate under supervision, not a target to copy blindly. For most people, a gentler deficit (300–500 calories below requirement) is healthier and much easier to sustain long-term.

## What you can take from this for yourself

If you want to translate "the Gabriela Cristea method" into your own steps, you don't need her exact menu, but the underlying principles: smaller portions, meals built around protein and vegetables, eliminating unnecessary calories (juices, frying, sweets), and regular movement. Exactly how much to cut, you can find out by putting numbers to your situation with the [calorie calculator](/calculator-calorii/).

## Frequently asked questions

### How many kilograms did Gabriela Cristea lose?
Around 25 kilograms, according to her public statements. Different sources mention figures between 20 and 25 kg, depending on when the statements were made.

### What diet did she follow?
Not a named diet — a low-calorie one. She reduced her intake to under approximately 1,000 calories per day during the weight loss period, with an emphasis on small portions, protein, salads, and soups, and she added exercise.

### Can I lose weight too at 1,000 calories per day?
Such a low threshold is not recommended for everyone and for the long term — it can lead to fatigue, muscle mass loss, and abandonment. For most people, a moderate deficit (300–500 calories below requirement) is safer and more sustainable. Ideally, set the threshold with a specialist.

### Did she use any medication or treatment?
From her public statements, no — she attributed the result to dietary discipline and movement, insisting she did not resort to expensive treatments or quick solutions.

The moral isn't that you need to eat what she eats, but that there's no secret to buy. The same boring rules — less on the plate, more movement, patience — work equally well for a celebrity and for you. The only difference is how consistently you apply them.
