When a celebrity loses visible weight, the public immediately wants the "recipe": what secret diet, what treatment, what trick did they use? In the case of Andra Măruță, her answer was suspiciously simple — so simple that many were disappointed. "I don't follow a particular diet," said the artist. "I kept discipline with food. Without discipline, I didn't manage." And in that seemingly ordinary sentence lies the whole secret.

Let's look at what she said publicly about the 10 kilograms she lost, what concrete changes she made, and why her approach, however uninteresting to those seeking miracles, is the only one that genuinely works long-term.

## How many kilograms and why she wanted to lose weight

Andra said she lost approximately **10 kilograms**. She admitted that at first it was hard, but that after the first 4–5 kilograms she felt she could continue and didn't stop, because she liked the results. That's an important detail: the first steps are always the hardest, and the moment you see the first proof on the scale gives you fuel to keep going.

The reason wasn't only aesthetic. The artist mentioned issues with **blood pressure**, and doctors told her that losing weight could help. In practice, the trigger came from a health reason — the kind of motivation that, statistically, keeps people on track better than a desire to fit into a dress.

## What she did, concretely

Her public statements reveal a few simple changes, none of them spectacular:

- **She reduced portions** — she ate, broadly, half of what she used to.
- **She gave up snacking between meals** — which she herself described as the "formula" that helped her most.
- **She added movement** — she started doing Pilates.
- **She sought a balance** between meals, rest, and exercise, without extreme restrictions and without starving.

Notice that no trendy diet name appears, no supplement, no injection. Just less on the plate, no nibbling between meals, and more movement. In technical terms, that means a consistent [calorie deficit](/dieta/deficit-caloric/) — the underlying mechanism of any weight loss. Andra got there without counting a single calorie, simply by eating less and less often.

It's worth highlighting the role of Pilates in her story, because the chosen movement says something. Pilates isn't a sport that burns an enormous number of calories — that's not where the weight loss comes from. But it tones, strengthens the core and back, and, perhaps most importantly, creates a habit and a routine. And habit is exactly what's missing in most failed weight loss attempts. Someone who sets aside time a few times a week for movement automatically makes better food choices for the rest of the day too — not because they burn calories at the gym, but because the mindset shifts.

## The myth her case debunks

The most widespread myth about celebrities who lose weight is that "they have resources we don't have" — expensive nutritionists, treatments, inaccessible solutions. Sometimes that's true. But Andra insisted on the exact opposite: no special diet, just discipline. Her main tool — portion control and giving up snacking — is free and available to anyone.

The myth is dangerous because it provides a convenient excuse: "if only I had her conditions too…" The reality is that the part that matters costs nothing. What "costs," and can't be bought, is the consistency to apply it every day, including the days you don't feel like it.

The second, more subtle myth concerns snacking. Many believe that "small, healthy" snacks between meals help with weight loss. The truth is nuanced: if you nibble constantly — a pastry here, a few biscuits there, an extra handful of nuts — you accumulate hundreds of calories you don't even register. Andra cut exactly that, and it's no coincidence she named it the key.

## Frequently asked questions

### How many kilograms did Andra Măruță lose?
Approximately 10 kilograms, according to her public statements, lost gradually through dietary discipline and movement.

### What diet did Andra follow?
None with a name. She said explicitly that she didn't follow any particular diet — she reduced portions (roughly by half), gave up snacking between meals, and started doing Pilates.

### Did she use pills or treatments?
From her public statements, no — she attributed the result entirely to discipline with food and exercise.

### Can I lose weight just by cutting snacks?
For many people, eliminating constant nibbling really does make a big difference, because that's where hundreds of uncounted calories hide. Combined with slightly smaller portions and movement, it's a simple and effective strategy.

### How long did it take to lose those 10 kilograms?
Andra described the process as gradual, not an overnight transformation — exactly how healthy weight loss should look. At a normal rate of 0.5–1 kg per week, 10 kilograms means somewhere between three and five months of consistency. The fact that it went slowly is, actually, a good sign: kilograms lost slowly, through habit change, are the ones that stay off, unlike those lost quickly through crash diets.

The moral isn't to copy what Andra ate, but to take away that there's no secret to buy. The same boring rules — less on the plate, no nibbling between meals, more movement — work equally well for a celebrity and for you. The only difference is how consistently you apply them.
