The biggest mistake when looking for "weight loss recipes" is thinking you need exotic, expensive ingredients — smoked salmon, quinoa, seeds you can only find in organic shops. The truth is exactly the opposite: the best weight loss foods are cheap, simple, and already in your kitchen. The secret lies not in special ingredients, but in the ratio between how much a food fills you up and how many calories it has. This is called **calorie density**, and it's the only rule that matters.

Below you have seven real recipes, each under 400 calories, built from ingredients you can find in any supermarket. None of them will leave you hungry an hour later.

## The principle behind any weight loss recipe

Before the recipes, the rule that ties them all together: a good weight loss meal has **high volume and low calories**. Vegetables, soups, lean proteins, and foods rich in water and fibre fill you up with few calories. Fats and starchy foods, though useful, are calorie-dense — use them as accents, not as the base. All the recipes below follow this principle, so you can eat until full while staying in a [calorie deficit](/dieta/deficit-caloric/).

## Breakfast (under 350 calories)

**1. Vegetable and cottage cheese omelette.** Two eggs beaten with tomatoes, peppers, and spinach, plus a tablespoon of cottage cheese. Protein and fibre that keep you full until lunch. ~280 kcal.

**2. Greek yogurt with oats and fruit.** 150 g Greek yogurt, two tablespoons of rolled oats, a handful of berries or a sliced apple. Creamy, naturally sweet, filling. ~300 kcal.

## Lunch (under 400 calories)

**3. Bean stew.** A hearty, filling bomb of fibre and plant protein. A portion of beans cooked with onion, carrot, and tomatoes keeps you full for hours. Cheap and perfect for weight loss. ~350 kcal per serving.

**4. Large salad with chicken breast.** 120 g grilled chicken breast, lettuce, cucumber, tomatoes, a tablespoon of olive oil, and lemon juice. Lots of food, few calories. ~380 kcal.

**5. Vegetable and lentil soup.** Lentils, carrot, celery, pepper, tomatoes. The lentils add protein and fibre that turn a light soup into a complete meal. ~300 kcal for a large serving.

## Dinner (under 400 calories)

**6. Baked fish with vegetables.** A piece of fish (cod, trout, hake) baked with zucchini, peppers, and tomatoes. Lean protein and vegetables, ready in 25 minutes. ~350 kcal.

**7. Bunless "burger."** A lean meat patty on a bed of lettuce, with tomato, cucumber, and a tablespoon of garlic yogurt instead of sauce. All the satisfaction of a burger, without the calorie-heavy bun. ~380 kcal.

## Tricks that make any recipe better for weight loss

Beyond the recipes, a few simple adjustments turn almost any meal into a diet-friendly one:

- **Start the meal with a soup or salad.** It fills you up with few calories and you naturally eat less of the main course.
- **Replace frying with baking or grilling.** You save 100–200 calories per serving without losing taste.
- **Swap fatty sauces for yogurt.** Garlic yogurt or herb yogurt replaces mayonnaise and sour cream at a fraction of the calories.
- **Double the vegetable portion, halve the side dish.** More sautéed cabbage, less rice — the same full plate, fewer calories.
- **Cook a big pot at once.** A soup or stew prepared for several days saves you from takeaway and unhealthy snacks when you don't feel like cooking.

## Frequently asked questions

### Do I need to count calories for every recipe?
At the start it helps to have a rough idea, to calibrate your eye. The recipes above are already designed to be filling and under 400 calories, so as long as you stick to reasonable portions, you don't need to obsessively weigh everything. See also [how many calories you need](/articole/cate-calorii-ca-sa-slabesti/).

### Can I lose weight eating ordinary, everyday food?
Yes, many everyday foods are excellent for weight loss — bean stew, vegetable soups, pea soup, stuffed cabbage (with lean meat). The problem comes with frying, huge portions, and excess bread, not with the food itself.

### How do I make food more filling without extra calories?
Add volume from vegetables and lean protein. An egg, some chicken breast, cottage cheese, or a tablespoon of lentils in a dish makes it fill you up much more, with few added calories.

### Are these recipes suitable for the whole family?
Yes. They're normal, balanced meals, not "special diet" food. The whole family can eat the same thing; you just pay attention to your portion and your side dish.

### How often can I eat the same recipes without getting bored?
The trick isn't infinite variety, but a few base recipes you rotate and vary with seasonings. The same chicken salad becomes different with basil and lemon, or with paprika and garlic. The same vegetable soup changes taste with a spoonful of yogurt or fresh herbs. It's easier to stick to a diet with 5–6 solid go-to meals you know by heart than to search for new recipes every day.

### Can I prepare the recipes in advance, for several days?
Yes, and it's one of the best strategies. A pot of soup or bean stew lasts several days in the fridge, and baked chicken breast or boiled eggs can be prepared once and used in multiple meals. When you have food ready in the fridge, you avoid takeaway and unhealthy snacks in exactly those moments of exhaustion when you give in most easily.

You don't need a chef's kitchen or expensive ingredients to lose weight. You need a few simple, filling recipes that you can repeat without getting bored. Start with two or three from the list above, find the ones you like, and build from there. The more your weight loss recipes resemble the normal food you already eat — just with more vegetables and less fat and bread — the better your chances of sticking with them long-term. And long-term is the only term that counts.
