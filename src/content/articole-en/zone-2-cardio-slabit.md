If you've spent years running as fast as you can or doing intense HIIT convinced that "harder =
better," you may have missed the most efficient way to burn fat. Zone 2 cardio — low-intensity,
sustained exercise where you can speak in full sentences — is one of the biggest fitness trends
in 2026, promoted by longevity researchers, cardiologists, and nutritionists alike. And it's
not a new trend: it's old science that has finally reached the general public.

## What zone 2 actually is

Exercise is divided into intensity zones based on heart rate. Zone 2 corresponds to approximately
**60–70% of your maximum heart rate** — a moderate effort where you can hold a conversation
but feel like you're working.

Simplified formula for maximum heart rate: **220 minus your age**. If you're 35, your maximum
heart rate is ~185 bpm. Zone 2 is between 111 and 130 bpm.

A simpler test than the watch: the conversation test. If you can talk in normal sentences, you're
in zone 2. If you're gasping and can't finish a sentence, you're already in zone 3 or above.

**Examples of activities that naturally fall in zone 2:**
- Brisk walking (not running)
- Relaxed cycling on flat terrain
- Swimming at a comfortable pace
- Elliptical at medium intensity
- Easy jogging (for trained individuals)

## Why zone 2 burns fat better

The body has two main energy sources: **glucose** (blood sugar and glycogen in muscles) and
**fat**. At high intensity, the body prefers glucose — it's faster. At low to moderate intensity,
the aerobic system has time to burn fat as the primary fuel.

Zone 2 is essentially the **zone of maximum fat burning** as a percentage of calories consumed.
With HIIT, you burn more calories per minute, but mostly from glucose. At zone 2, you burn
fewer calories per minute, but the percentage coming from fat is much higher.

Long term, zone 2 also improves **mitochondrial efficiency** — mitochondria are the cellular
"engines" that convert fat into energy. More functional mitochondria means you burn fat more
easily even at rest.

## Zone 2 vs HIIT: which is better for weight loss

There's no absolute winner — it depends on the context.

**HIIT** (High Intensity Interval Training) burns more calories in less time and produces the
EPOC effect (you burn calories for several hours after the workout). But it has downsides: it
heavily stresses the nervous system, requires 48-hour recovery, and many people can't sustain
it regularly without injury or quitting.

**Zone 2** burns fewer calories per session, but you can do 5–6 sessions per week without
recovery problems. Long term, the total calories burned is often higher than with HIIT. Plus
it improves basal metabolism and long-term cardiovascular health.

**Practical conclusion:** if you're a beginner or have too little time for recovery, zone 2
is more sustainable. If you're trained, a combination of 80% zone 2 and 20% HIIT is close to
ideal — the method used by elite athletes and longevity researchers like Peter Attia.

## How much zone 2 do you need to lose weight

**Effective minimum:** 150 minutes per week (5 sessions × 30 min or 3 sessions × 50 min).
Studies show 150 min/week of moderate cardio is associated with significant reduction in
visceral fat (belly fat).

**Optimal for weight loss and metabolic health:** 180–250 min/week. If you have time and
patience, longer sessions (45–60 min) are more effective than short ones.

**Important:** zone 2 works best if you haven't eaten recently. A morning session on an empty
stomach (or several hours after your last meal) maximizes fat burning.

## How to include zone 2 in everyday life

No gym needed. Zone 2 is compatible with ordinary life:

- **Walk to work or shopping** faster than usual (without running)
- **Daily walks** of 45–60 minutes at a brisk pace
- **Bicycle** on flat roads as transportation
- **Stairs instead of elevator**, multiplied throughout the day

If you prefer the gym, treadmill, elliptical, or stationary bike at medium intensity are
perfect. Set them to a level where you can read, talk, or watch something on TV — that's zone 2.

## The most common mistake: going too intense

Most people who want to do "easy" cardio still end up in zone 3 without realizing it. If you
feel breathless, can't speak comfortably, or your heart rate exceeds 75% of maximum, you've
left zone 2. Lower the intensity.

It's counterintuitive, but sometimes it's harder to stay in zone 2 than to go at maximum
intensity — especially for trained people, whose body "wants" to go faster.

## Zone 2 and diet: what combination works

Zone 2 alone, without attention to diet, gives modest results. Combined with a moderate caloric
deficit diet, results multiply.

It works best with a protein-rich diet (to preserve muscle mass) and moderate in carbohydrates
(to maintain available glycogen for activity, but without excess). No special diet is needed —
any healthy balanced diet complements zone 2.

## Frequently asked questions

### Does zone 2 work for sedentary people?
Yes, and possibly even better than for trained individuals. In sedentary people, the body has
more fat to mobilize and less efficient mitochondria — improvements are visible quickly. A brisk
30-min daily walk is enough as a starting point.

### Do I absolutely need a heart rate watch?
Not mandatory. The conversation test is enough to guide intensity. If you want precision, a
heart rate watch or even a phone app can help.

### Can I do zone 2 if I have joint problems?
Yes. Swimming, cycling, and elliptical don't stress joints as much as running. They're excellent
options for people with knee or hip problems.

### Does zone 2 reduce belly fat?
You can't choose where you lose fat (spot-reduction is a myth), but zone 2 reduces visceral fat
(the fat around organs, in the belly) more efficiently than other forms of exercise — there are
specific studies on this.

Zone 2 cardio isn't about performance — it's about patience. Thirty minutes of brisk walking
daily, five times a week, is more effective long term than an hour of HIIT three times a week,
with exhausting recovery between. Simple, accessible, and science-based.
